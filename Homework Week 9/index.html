The Effects of Air Pollution

by Vincent Lu

Introduction to Air Pollution

Air pollution are substances in the air that are harmful to human health. Air pollution can come from indoor and outdoor environments and affects the entire world. This visualisation has been made to highlight the effects of air pollution on the globe.

Air pollution can be anthropogenic (human-made) and therefore should be monitored and controlled to reduce damage from human sources.

Motor vehicles, industrial facilities and commercial and domestic activities are common sources of air pollution.

In 2017, almost 5 million deaths were from Air pollution.

Types of Air Pollution

Taking a closer look at the individual types of air pollution and comparing between countries, indoor pollution is by far the greatest cause of death. Indoor air pollution refers to pollution around buildings and in the home. Common sources are tobacco smoke, mold, pollen, household products and certain building materials such as asbestos.

Outdoor Particle Pollution refers to extremely small particles and liquid droplets in the air. Common sources are motor vehicles, wood burning heaters and industrial facilities.

Outdoor Ozone Pollution comes from sunlight interacting with the emissions particles in the air and therefore has the same sources as mentioned above in outdoor particle pollution.

Air Pollution Trends

Since 2010, efforts have been made to reduce air pollution through long-term policies and other recent attempts. Despite this, continued efforts should be made to continue this trend.

There are still large numbers of people that are exposed to harmful levels of air pollution.

Conclusion

This visualisation highlights the effects of air pollution and the importance of monitoring these levels as large amounts of people are exposed to and die to air pollution each year. The visualisation also highlights the enormous impact countries in Africa and Asia have on global air pollution effects and shows that while air pollution has been decreasing over the more recents years, further efforts should continue to be made to alleviate the problems of air pollution.
